Policy Background The following policy clauses are taken directly from the Yonsei University Guidelines for Students on the Use of Generative AI, used in this study to explore how students interpret AI-related academic policies.

Yonsei University — Guidelines for Students on the Use of Generative AI

Clause 2. Verify accuracy and reliability of AI outputs. Due to its inherent nature, generative AI may produce false or misleading information. It is essential to critically verify and fact-check its outputs.

● Understand the limitations and potential drawbacks of generative AI, and evaluate its outputs with a critical and questioning mindset.

● Always keep your learning objectives in mind. Emphasise self-directed learning and inquiry, and be mindful not to become overly dependent on AI tools.

Clause 3. Act ethically and remain aware of potential biases in AI outputs. Although generative AI is designed to reject unethical prompts, indirect or rephrased questions may still lead to the generation of inappropriate or unethical content. Users should exercise caution, discretion, and a strong sense of responsibility.

● When AI is trained on biased or discriminatory data, it may produce skewed or harmful outputs that could inadvertently influence your academic work or research outcomes. Always remain vigilant and evaluate AI-generated content with critical scrutiny.

Clause 5. Always provide proper citation when using generative AI tools. If students use generative AI in their academic work, they must provide clear and accurate citations. Submitting AI-generated content without proper attribution or using it without modification may be considered plagiarism or academic misconduct.

● Provide clear citations including the specific model used, the prompt entered, and the name of the platform.

Example (APA): OpenAI. (Year). ChatGPT (Month Day version) [Large language model]. https://chat.openai.com

Example (MLA): "Exact prompt you used" prompt. ChatGPT, Day Month version, OpenAI, Day Month Year, chat.openai.com.

Your Analysis

Address these 5 dimensions:

  1. Behavioral Impact — How might this policy change how students work?
  2. Decision-Making — How does it affect decisions about using AI?
  3. Well-being — What emotional or psychological effects might it have?
  4. Benefits — What are the genuine benefits for students?
  5. Potential Drawbacks — What are realistic concerns or limitations?
